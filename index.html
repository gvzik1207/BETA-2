合氣道 (Aikido Club)

Club Adviser – Gelkoff A. Calmerin

The Tay Tung Aikido Club is a martial arts club inspired by the philosophy of Harmony. Aikido directly translates as The Way of Harmony.

Rules

Attend all scheduled training unless with a valid excuse.
Always observe proper hygiene.
Always clean and take care of Dojo (Training hall), training equipment and uniform.
Always respect the Sensei, classmates, and guests.
Extend training discipline beyond the Dojo.
Be responsible for personal development.
Help others develop themselves.
Always inform the Sensei of urgent and important matters.

Programs

Keiko - basic training
Zazen – meditation
Ashi sabaki – footwork
Ukemi – breakfall
Tachi waza – standing techniques
Suwari waza – seated techniques
Hanmi handachi waza – seated vs standing techniques
Jiu Waza, Randori – variations, freestyle
Ken-Jo Suburi and Kata – sword and staff training
Kyu grade exams
Martial Arts Seminars – ex. Visayas Aikido Fellowship

Armonia Music Club

Club Adviser – Harold Christian G. Montoya

The Armonia Music Club was established to provide dedicated student musicians a space to explore and enhance their musical talents beyond the classroom. "Armonia" means harmony, reflecting the club’s goal of nurturing not only musical skill but also teamwork, discipline, and emotional expression. This club serves as a platform for training, ensemble performance, and personal artistic growth.

Rules

Attend all scheduled rehearsals, performances, and activities unless with valid excuse.
Maintain and care for all instruments and music materials.
Respect the conductor, adviser, peers, and guest mentors at all times.
Come prepared and on time for all rehearsals and events.
Extend discipline and professionalism beyond the music room.
Practice regularly and take responsibility for musical improvement.
Support fellow members in their musical journey.
Communicate promptly with the adviser regarding concerns and absences.

Programs

Music Creation and Arrangement
Rehearsals for New Repertoire
Performance Engagements
Musical Collaboration
Creative Development Workshops

Book Lover’s Club

Club Advisers – Mrs. Mary Jane M. Zarceno, Mrs. Yulita Q. Suarez

A Book Lover’s Club is a community of students who share a passion for reading, discussing books and also an integration of craftsmanship. Members meet regularly to explore different types of books, exchange ideas, and deepen their understanding of literature. The club fosters intellectual growth, social connection, a lifelong love of reading and developing their skills in Arts and Crafts.

Rules

Be Respectful – Always listen when someone is speaking. Respect others’ opinions, even if they’re different from yours.
Take Turns – Raise your hand and wait for your turn to speak during discussion.
Be Kind – Use kind words and actions.
Handle Books with Care – No tearing, scribbling, or damaging books.
Come Prepared – Bring your book, notebook, or any needed materials to every meeting.
Participate Fully – Join all activities, share your thoughts, and try your best even if you're shy!
Use Inside Voices – Speak politely so everyone can enjoy reading and talking together.
Stay on Topic – Keep conversations focused on the book or activity unless your teacher or club leader says otherwise.
Clean up – Help tidy the room or return materials to their proper place after meetings.
Have fun and Be Creative! – Enjoy your time in the club, be open to learning, and let your imagination shine.

Programs

Read-Aloud Sessions; Book Talks or Show & Tell; Silent Reading Time; Book-Themed Crafts; Story Retelling or Role-Play; Puppet Making and Shows; Club Bulletin Board; Author Spotlights; Book Swap; Library Visits or Virtual Storytime.

CHEFS (Creative Hands Exploring Foods and Sweets)

Club Adviser – Mrs. Angela D. Dumay, LPT
Assistant Club Adviser – Ms. Ma. Catherine B. Llanto, LPT

The CHEFS Club was established to give young learners a chance to explore their passion for baking. It was created as a fun and interactive space where students from Grades 4 to 6 can develop creativity, patience, and essential life skills through baking. The club introduces basic baking techniques and allows students to enjoy hands-on experiences in preparing cookies, cupcakes, and other beginner-friendly sweets.

Rules

Attend all scheduled meetings and baking sessions unless with a valid excuse.
Always observe cleanliness and hygiene in food preparation.
Be respectful and cooperative with fellow members and the adviser.
Share baking tools and ingredients responsibly.
Clean up workstations before leaving the venue.
Follow all safety procedures in handling equipment and ingredients.
Submit requirements and contributions (if any) on time.
Maintain a positive attitude and encourage others to participate.

Programs

Weekly baking sessions (e.g., cookies, brownies, cupcakes); “Sweet Treats Day” – Mini bake sale showcasing student creations; Inter-grade friendly bake-off; Guest demos by chefs/teachers or senior high school students; Holiday-themed baking (e.g., Halloween cookies, Christmas cupcakes).

Eureka!

Club Adviser – Mr. Christian Castro

The Eureka club was built in the year 2010. It all began when a group of students and an enthusiastic adviser with a big dream and clear vision of establishing a science club for Tay Tung students came to life. As years go by, more students draw on the enthusiasm and promise of hands-on experiments, educational exposure, camaraderie, and love for nature.

Rules

Respect for everyone: Treat all club members with respect. Behaviour not adhering to the school’s core values will not be tolerated.
Active participation: We encourage every member to actively participate in all club activities.
Punctuality and Responsibility: Arrive on time for meetings and events. Take responsibility for your actions to the club.

Programs

Mangrove and Tree Planting.
Coastal clean-up.
Medical missions.
Community Outreach.
School beautification/Green Spaces.
Educational trip.
Symposiums/Seminars.

BTTHS Serviam Club

Club Adviser – Danny Israel G. Saloria

The Tay Tung Serviam Club is a values-based service club encouraging volunteerism and servant leadership.

Rules

The club is open to all students from Grades 7–12 who submit a signed commitment form and attend the official orientation.
Members are expected to actively take part in all club activities.
Members must be on time and present in all required meetings, events, and volunteer duties. Absences must be communicated in advance to the officer in charge.
Members must be part of the official group chat or platform to receive updates and instructions.

Programs

Serviam Encounter; Pagdihon Formation Series; Project Yuhom; Serviam Work Camp; Serviam Quest; Community Immersion; Outreach involvement.

Young Designers Club

Club Adviser – Mina Retita-Uhlmann

The Young Designers Club is a creative space for Grades 4 to 12 students who love design, innovation, and hands-on learning. Guided by design thinking, members turn ideas into real projects that solve problems and benefit the community.

Rules

Members must be committed. Three consecutive absences from group meetings will be grounds for termination of membership.
Members and Leads must be responsible for their individual or team projects.
Members must follow all BTTHS Rules and Regulations.

Programs

Design Sprints and Innovation Challenges; Hands-on Maker Projects; Passion-Based Mini-Workshops and Skill-building Activities; Community Design and Outreach Initiatives; Showcases and Exhibitions.
