合氣道 (Aikido Club)

Club Adviser – Gelkoff A. Calmerin

The Tay Tung Aikido Club is a martial arts club inspired by the philosophy of Harmony. Aikido directly translates as The Way of Harmony.

Rules

  • Attend all scheduled training unless with a valid excuse.
  • Always observe proper hygiene.
  • Always clean and take care of Dojo, training equipment, and uniform.
  • Always respect the Sensei, classmates, and guests.
  • Extend training discipline beyond the Dojo.
  • Be responsible for personal development.
  • Help others develop themselves.
  • Always inform the Sensei of urgent and important matters.

Programs

  • Keiko - basic training
  • Zazen – meditation
  • Ashi sabaki – footwork
  • Ukemi – breakfall
  • Tachi waza – standing techniques
  • Suwari waza – seated techniques
  • Hanmi handachi waza – seated vs standing techniques
  • Jiu Waza, Randori – variations, freestyle
  • Ken-Jo Suburi and Kata – sword and staff training
  • Kyu grade exams
  • Martial Arts Seminars – e.g., Visayas Aikido Fellowship

Armonia

Club Adviser – Harold Christian G. Montoya

The Armonia Music Club provides dedicated student musicians a space to explore and enhance their musical talents. "Armonia" means harmony, reflecting the club’s goal of nurturing musical skill, teamwork, and emotional expression.

Rules

  • Attend all scheduled rehearsals, performances, and activities unless with valid excuse.
  • Maintain and care for all instruments and music materials.
  • Respect the conductor, adviser, peers, and guest mentors at all times.
  • Come prepared and on time for all rehearsals and events.
  • Extend discipline and professionalism beyond the music room.
  • Practice regularly and take responsibility for musical improvement.
  • Support fellow members in their musical journey.
  • Communicate promptly with the adviser regarding concerns and absences.

Programs

  • Music Creation and Arrangement
  • Rehearsals for New Repertoire
  • Performance Engagements
  • Musical Collaboration
  • Creative Development Workshops

Book Lover’s Club

Club Advisers – Mrs. Mary Jane M. Zarceno, Mrs. Yulita Q. Suarez

The Book Lover’s Club is a community of students who share a passion for reading, discussing books, and crafting. It fosters intellectual growth, social connection, and a lifelong love of reading and art.

Rules

  • Be respectful and listen when someone is speaking.
  • Take turns and wait for your turn to speak.
  • Be kind and use polite language.
  • Handle books with care.
  • Come prepared for every meeting.
  • Participate fully in all activities.
  • Use inside voices during discussions.
  • Stay on topic and clean up after meetings.

Programs

  • Read-Aloud Sessions
  • Book Talks or Show & Tell
  • Silent Reading Time
  • Book-Themed Crafts
  • Story Retelling or Role-Play
  • Puppet Making and Shows
  • Club Bulletin Board
  • Author Spotlights
  • Book Swap
  • Library Visits or Virtual Storytime

BTTHS Serviam Club

Club Adviser – Danny Israel G. Saloria

The Serviam Club promotes leadership, service, and compassion through active involvement and community programs inspired by the spirit of Great Harmony (大同).

Rules

  • Open to Grades 7–12 students who submit a commitment form and attend orientation.
  • Participate actively in all club activities.
  • Be punctual and communicate absences in advance.
  • Join the official communication platform for updates.
  • Fulfill assigned committee or leadership roles responsibly.
  • Uphold respect, integrity, and responsibility at all times.
  • Earn leadership roles through dedication and service.
  • Embody volunteerism, compassion, and unity.

Programs

  • Serviam Encounter
  • Pagdihon Formation Series
  • Project Yuhom
  • Serviam Work Camp
  • Serviam Quest
  • Community Immersion
  • Outreach Involvement

CHEFS (Creative Hands Exploring Foods and Sweets)

Club Adviser – Mrs. Angela D. Dumay, LPT
Assistant Club Adviser – Ms. Ma. Catherine B. Llanto, LPT

The CHEFS Club gives young learners an opportunity to explore their passion for baking while developing creativity, patience, and life skills.

Rules

  • Attend all scheduled meetings and baking sessions unless excused.
  • Always observe cleanliness and hygiene in food preparation.
  • Be respectful and cooperative with fellow members and advisers.
  • Share baking tools and ingredients responsibly.
  • Clean up workstations before leaving.
  • Follow all safety procedures when handling equipment.
  • Submit requirements or contributions on time.
  • Maintain a positive and encouraging attitude.

Programs

  • Weekly baking sessions
  • “Sweet Treats Day” – Mini bake sale
  • Inter-grade friendly bake-off
  • Guest demos by chefs or students
  • Holiday-themed baking sessions

Young Designers Club

Club Adviser – Mina Retita-Uhlmann

The Young Designers Club is a creative space for students who love design, innovation, and hands-on learning. It serves as the youth arm of BTTHS’ Design, Research, and Innovation Learning Laboratory (DRILL).

Rules

  • Members must be committed; three consecutive absences may result in termination.
  • Be responsible for individual and team projects.
  • Follow all BTTHS Rules and Regulations.
  • Serve as official participants in DRILL’s Project-Based Learning Program.

Programs

  • Design Sprints and Innovation Challenges
  • Hands-on Maker Projects
  • Mini-Workshops and Skill-building Activities
  • Community Design and Outreach
  • Showcases and Exhibitions
